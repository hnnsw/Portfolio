Say hello to Fairdi!

Turn old into new in a sustainable way.

Role

Wireframing, Prototyping, User Interviews, User Testing

Duration

October 2019 - January 2020

Team

Thomas Raffetzeder, Nicolas Rosenecker, Daniel Saiz, Hannes Wolfmayr

Tools

Axure

Background

As a semester project, we had the task to concept a mobile prototype that would solve a sustainability problem.

Problem

It is always rewarding to treat yourself to something new. But what happens to the things we replace? We collect more and more things and forget that some used items still work without problems and only our expectations have changed. In this way we are producing more and more “trash”, which consists of objects that may not be useful, beautiful or practical for us anymore.

These useless things can gain a lot of value for us again, only we rarely know how we can do this or we are too lazy because it looks like too much work. However, this is rarely the case.

This is exactly where we intervene and offer people ideas with our app to create new things out of old things. We provide contacts to repair broken things or donate unwanted things. Because for other people, these things can be still useful, something long-awaited or even an increase in the quality of life. For every kind of old device, as a refrigerator, bike, tablet, smartphone and many more, we offer a suitable section in our app. This thoughts form the three basic sections of our app: Upcycling, Repairing, Recycling

Personas

Persona skeletons

As a first step, research and brainstorming in the team were used to find possible statements and problems from the target group. In order to get as real statements as possible, we also interviewed friends and relatives and searched for suitable studies on the Internet that represent and prove our problem.

Result (Personas)

Based on the persona skeletons we have formed 2 personas. The first persona has a constant urge for new technology and therefore has many unused devices. The second persona has a very high level of environmental awareness and tries to keep its carbon footprint as low as possible.

Wireframing

After a bunch of user interviews and brainstorming sessions we made the first steps for the UI.

Prototyping

In total, the prototype went through three feedback loops in which potential users, study colleagues and professors evaluated the prototype.

Outcome

We decided on a design that is already known to users. The mixture of map views and grid views should enable users to easily navigate through the app. The app is so colorful because the evaluations have shown that the separation of the sections is otherwise not clear and causes confusion. All in all, the idea and conception were very well received. There is a lot of interest, especially among younger users.

Learnings

I am absolutely grateful that I got to know new techniques and tools in this project. I was able to expand my knowledge, particularly in the area of ​​user research, user interviews and prototyping. As I normally prefer Sketch and Figma as my number one prototyping tools, it was exciting to get an insight into Axure.
